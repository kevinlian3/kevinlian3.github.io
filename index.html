British Columbia is building a fracking industry in the northeast corner of the province. As a group of physicians and scientists, we are extremely concerned about the impact of this project.

To understand what is happening, let's start by learning about LNG

Liquified Natural Gas, or LNG, involves the use of hydraulic fracturing (fracking) to access gas reserves that were previously inaccesssible. Fracking involves the high pressure injection of water, sand, and chemicals into, driller wells, which fractures the earth to release gas deposits. Almost all of the gas currently used in BC households and industries has been extracted using fracking technology.
