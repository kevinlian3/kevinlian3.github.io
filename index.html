There is nothing natural about natural gas.

From its production, transportation, to its eventual use, natural gas is destroying our planet and our health. Yet, British Columbia is becoming a major producer of natural gas.


Why is Natural Gas

unhealthy

Let us start by clarifying the term "natural gas".

Natural gas is mostly composed of methane. It is also a fossil fuel formed from the decay of organisms that lived millions of years ago. Most natural gas deposits are found deep underground like other fossil fuels.

In fact two of North America's largest gas reserves are located in British Columbia several kilometeres underground. To access these deposits, gas companies use a technique called hydraulic fracturing, or more commonly known as "fracking".

How does fracking work exactly?


Fracking is a technique to extract natural gas from hard-to-exploit rock formations deep into the ground. Fracking is not new, but it is becoming a widespread technology in BC for gas production.



Mounting evidence shows that fracking poses serious threats to our health, environment, and climate future. Here’s a look at the process of fracking and the perils it presents.

As the natural gas reserves can sit more three kilometres underground, the process begins with the drilling of a long vertical well that extends deep into the earth.

Once the well reaches the target gas formation, drilling then turns horizontal and can continue for a kilometre or more.

Now comes the actual fracking part. Afterwards, water, sand and chemicals are injected into the rock at high pressure. The fluids crack open the shale rock, and the sand holds those cracks open.

From the shale, natural gas then begins flowing and up out of the well, where it is eventually shipped to consumers via pipeline.
